About Nour

I'm Nour al-Zaghloul (thus the webpage). I am a Palestinian lesbian (and other queer) transfemme researcher. I obtained a Bachelor’s in Psychology (specializing in development) with a minor in education from the University of California, Davis. I have worked in a professional capacity in formal learning environments, academia, and informal learning environments.

I started out working in various positions (both volunteered and paid) in school districts across Northern California where I really honed my ability to work with children of all ages and demographics.

Then after getting a taste of research, I worked as a research assistant and later a lab manager for various labs at the University of California, Davis. Here was my formal introduction into academia and research in general. I developed skills in research design, statistical analysis, and the dissemination of findings.

Afterwards, I moved to be the lab manager for a new research lab at Carnegie Mellon University. This gained me my specialization in creating and managing large scale projects as I had to build the lab from scratch. Everything put into production was personally developed and hand-coded.

Finally, I moved into the informal learning world working as a researcher for the Children’s Museum of Pittsburgh. At the museum I gained experience on both the more qualitative side of things as well as research application rather than theory.

I currently reside in Pittsburgh, PA with my wonderful wife and two lovely cats.

CV & Resume

I am a research and data specialist who has over 12 years working with children, 8 years of programming professional solutions in many languages, and 7 years managing large scale projects in a myriad of contexts.

I specialize in studying how systems interact to create changes over time. Originally, I was educated in developmental psychology focusing on emerging cognitive systems, but over time I have expanded onto the study of dynamic systems no matter their context. Currently I mainly do research focused around Diversity, Equity, Inclusivity, and Accessibility in informal learning spaces.
